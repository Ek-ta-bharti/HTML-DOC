Understanding Joint Pain with Age

As we age, the wear and tear on our joints can lead to various forms of joint pain. This is often due to the gradual loss of cartilage, which acts as a cushion between bones. Over time, this cartilage breaks down, leading to increased friction and pain in the joints.

Common conditions associated with aging include osteoarthritis, where the cartilage deteriorates and causes joint pain and stiffness. Additionally, conditions like rheumatoid arthritis can also develop, where the body's immune system attacks the joints, causing inflammation and pain.

It's essential to stay active and maintain a healthy lifestyle to manage and reduce the risk of joint pain as you age. Regular exercise, a balanced diet, and appropriate medical interventions can help maintain joint health and alleviate symptoms.
